WATERFALLS IN MADHYA PRADESH



1.Bhoora Khon Waterfall: Rich Greenery

Situated close to the MadhavSagar Lake at a height of 25 meters, the BhooraKhon cascaded waterfall offers you a superb characteristic encounter. Along with this, the look of the fall appears bewitching and satisfying to the eyes. There is an antiquated symbol of Lord Shiva situated at the back of waterfall rendering the blend of both nature and religion. Full of rich greenery, these waterfalls offer you a restoring escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. The monsoon season is one of the best seasons to visit this waterfall for the rejuvenating scenic beauty.


Timings:

08:00 AM to 06:00 PM(All days)

Location:

Shivpuri, Madhya Pradesh 473551

Entry Fee:

Indians: There are no charges to visit.

Camera Fee: None



2.Pandava Waterfalls: Serene Escape

The Pandava waterfall is a natural attraction that draws attention more frequently. Located at a height of 30 meters from the land in the middle of Panna, this sight of flowing water around the edges mesmerizes the visitor’s subconscious mind. This fall is named after the legendary Hindu epic – Mahabharat. It is believed that the Pandava brothers visited this pool. This legend is what acts as a magnet for most of the tourists.


Timings:

06:00 AM to 06:00 PM(All days)

Location:

NH39, Nahri, Madhya Pradesh 488001

Entry Fee:

Indians: INR 50 per head

Camera Fee: None



3.Raneh Falls: Everlasting Beauty

This natural waterfall is situated at a height of 30 meters from the ground and on the Ken river located in the district of Chattarpur, Madhya Pradesh. The everlasting beauty of the landscape is what makes this place a perfect spot for photography sessions. Waterfalls of Madhya Pradesh are more bewitching during the rainy season as the overwhelming deluge fills the cascades full. This is one of the most attractive tourist locations across the country.


Timings:

09:00 AM to 06:00 PM(Except Wednesday, it is open till noon)

Location:

Raneh Falls Rd, Dhoguwan, Madhya Pradesh 471606

Entry Fee:

Indians: INR 15per head

Camera Fee: None



4.Bahuti Falls: Indian Niagara

Being the highest waterfall in MP at the height 142 meters, the Bahuti Fall sets a record across the country and is often famed as the ‘Indian Niagara’. Being one of the exotic yet secluded places in Madhya Pradesh, the waterfall has become a perfect example of a nick center. This waterfall redefines the term ‘spellbound’ when an individual witnesses the mesmerizing view of the falling water.


Timings:

9 AM to 5 PM

Location:

Rewa, Sumida Kalan, Madhya Pradesh 486331

Entry Fee:

Indians: INR 15per head

Camera Fee: None



5.Kapil Dhara Waterfall: Vindhya Vaibhav

The Kapil Dhara waterfall is situated 6 km from the northwest of Narmada Kund at a height of 30.48 meters. This fall is the first waterfall from the Narmada river. Kapil Muni lived at this place according to sacred scripture. As indicated by ‘Vindhya Vaibhav’, Kapil Muni performed serious rigor here and also composed the ‘Sankhya theory’ (mathematics theory) at this spot. The encompassing mountains possess many caves where saints have done their profound practices related to spirituality.


Timings:

10:00 AM to 05:00 PM

Location:

Amarkantak, Madhya Pradesh, India

Entry Fee:

Indians: There are no charges to visit.

Camera Fee: None



6.Tincha Falls: Milky Streams

One can easily fall in love with this picturesque beauty of Tincha falls. This place is a good way off 25 kilometers from Indore city. Tincha Falls is a perfect package full of stunning location; it’s a valley with an impeccable natural beauty of canyon with a gorge. This fall got its name from the nearby village named Tincha. Tincha Falls is a popular destination among the sightseers, as the magnificence of by virtue of the magnificent falls that resembles a milky stream. These falls are certainly a perfect destination for picnics, adventure trips or trekking excursions.

08:00 AM to 06:00 PM

Berchha, Madhya Pradesh 452020

7. Patalpani Falls: Refreshing Weekend Getaway

Patalpani Falls is one of the most visited waterfalls in Madhya Pradesh and its proximity to Indore make it a popular weekend getaway spot for the people looking for quick escapes from their city life. The best time to plan a visit here is between July to October as monsoon welcomes gushing water to the stream that falls from the height of 150 feet.

9:00 AM – 6:00 PM

Location:

Kekariya Dabri, 35 km from Indore, Madhya Pradesh

Entry Fee:

Indians: There are no charges to visit.

Camera Fee: None



8. Dhuandhar Falls: Mesmerising Site

Dhuandhar waterfall is also known as Smoke cascade and is one of the most famous waterfalls in Madhya Pradesh. Water cascades from the height of 98 feet, forming a gorge called Marble Rocks. If you love exploring the beauty of nature then you must surely not miss out on this beautiful place while staying in Madhya Pradesh. This mesmerizing site is 35 km from Jabalpur and welcomes maximum visitors between September to March.


Timings:

06:00 AM to 06:00 PM

Location:

Bhedaghat, 30 km from Jabalpur, Madhya Pradesh

Entry Fee:

Indians:There are no charges to visit.

Camera Fee: None



9.Rajat Pratap Falls: Horse-Tail Type Waterfall

Rajat Pratap waterfall, popularly known as Silver Falls is a horse-tail type waterfall in Madhya Pradesh that is perched at an altitude of 351 feet. It is amongst the most gorgeous waterfalls in Madhya Pradesh and is frequently visited by adventure enthusiasts indulged in trekking, camping, and other activities. The waterfall holds religious significance and you will come across exciting stories related to Hindu mythology on visiting this refreshing spot. This is one of the most beautiful waterfall in MP.


Timings:

6:00 AM to 5:00 PM

Location:

Apsara Vihar, near Panchmarhi, Madhya Pradesh

Entry Fee:

Indians:There are no charges to visit.

Camera Fee: None



10. Keoti Falls: Continuation Of Bahuti Falls

Keoti Falls is a beautiful continuation of Bahuti Falls and this majestic waterfall in Madhya Pradesh descends from Tons river over the plateau of Rewa making it a spellbinding site for nature admirers. You can also indulge in adventurous water sports activities here on your excursion to this lively as well as a stunning waterfall located at a distance of 46 km from Rewa. You can take a taxi or local bus from City centre to reach here.


Timings:

06:00 AM to 06:00 PM

Location:

Keoti, Madhya Pradesh

Entry Fee:

Indians: There are no charges to visit.

Camera Fee: None



11. Bee Falls: Gorgeous Sunsets

Bee Falls is another mesmerizing waterfall in MP that makes visitors fall in love with its alluring beauty. This waterfall is also a popular sunset point and you will see numerous visitors heading to Bee Falls especially during late afternoons to capture the eyeful sights of the surround ambiance. The waterfall is located at a walkable distance from Apsara Vihar and Pachmarhi bus stand is approximately 3 km from here. The place is also believed to be related to mythological tales and you will come across numerous stories related to Pandavas and their exile while exploring the site.


Timings:

06:00 AM to 06:00 PM

Location:

Pachmarhi, Madhya Pradesh

Entry Fee:

Indians: INR 15per head

Camera Fee: None



12. Pawa Falls: Peaceful Ambiance

At Pawa Falls in Pohri water cascades from a height of 100 feet into a tank and the place holds religious significance among Hindu pilgrims who often come here for holy baths. The tank is called Pawa Kund and is truly a delightful place for divers. You can indulge in water sports here such as snorkelling or scuba diving but you need to carry your gear with you as equipment are not available at the location.


Timings:

06:00 AM to 06:00 PM

Location:

Basai, 40 km from Shivpuri, Madhya Pradesh

Entry Fee:

Indians: There are no charges to visit.

Camera Fee: None



13. Purwa Falls: Spell Binding Vistas

Purwa Falls is another stunning place to be explored in Rewa, Madhya Pradesh. Here water cascades from a height 300 feet offering spell binding vistas. The place is believed to be associated with the mythological epic, Ramayana. The site is quite popular among tourists staying in Rewa and Satna and you can also see numerous pilgrims indulged in holy baths here. This is surely one of the most prominent waterfalls in Madhya Pradesh.


Timings:

10 AM to 5:30 PM

Location:

Rewa district, Madhya Pradesh

Entry Fee:

Indians: INR 10per head

Camera Fee: None



14. Sultangarh Falls: Natural Cascades Amid Rocky Terrain

Sultangarh Waterfall is nestled near Shivpuri amid the lush green valleys having serene ambiance. These are the natural cascades, originating from River Parvati as water gushes in between rocky terrain and disarranged trees. It is a popular picnic spot and you will see locals of the nearby areas spending refreshing hours with their friends and family members.

16. Dhanora Falls: For Nature Admirers

Dhanora Falls, also known as Lilahi waterfall is located amidst lush green valleys in Chhindwara district, Madhya Pradesh. This gorgeous waterfall is formed by the descent of Kanha River. The place is surrounded by vibrant flora with melodious chirps of birds beautifying the environment. The waterfall is located on the road to Devgarh and its proximity to Narayan Ghat makes it a hotspot of visitors. You can easily reach here by hiring a taxi or boarding a local bus. If you want to capture the best of this place then you must plan a visit between July to January.


Timings:

06:00 AM to 06:00 PM

Location:

Chhindwara district, Madhya Pradesh

Entry Fee:

Indians: There are no charge to visit.

Camera Fee: None
